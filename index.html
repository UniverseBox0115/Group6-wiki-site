Biosynthesis of an Alternative to Spray Paint for Containers - PHA

Paint manufacturing (occupational exposure) is on the list of category 3 carcinogens.

The protective equipment for container painting workers is grossly inadequate; workers are expected to wear chemical-resistant gloves and suits, but ordinary leather gloves and homemade plastic sheeting are used instead. This situation puts workers' health at risk for a long time.

Polyhydroxyalkanoates (PHAs) are microbially produced polyesters that can be synthesised by bacterial fermentation of sugars or lipids. It serves as both an energy source and a carbon store. PHAs have advantages over other paint alternatives in terms of degradability, biocompatibility, processing and moulding, low-temperature performance, VOC emissions and renewability of raw materials.

Pathway 1:
Synthesis of medium and long chain PHA under low nitrogen conditions.

Pathway 2:
Inhibition of cell division promotes cell elongation and self-sedimentation
